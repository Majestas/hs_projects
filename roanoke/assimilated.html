Assimilated into Native American Tribe

The first possible theory is the colonists assimilated into the nearby Native American tribe. The single word “CROATOAN” that was carved into one of the posts may have referenced the nearby tribe that shares the same name.

Assimlated due to Unknown Conditions

The settlers probably joined the Croatoan Tribe because these people were not used to living in the conditions in their new location. There was also evidence of a drought, based on journal entries made by White.

Native Americans with European Traits

It is also evidenced that they assimilated to the native tribes because it is impossible for the natives to have gained european traits without european descent, which is most likely from the Roanoke colonists.

However, Natives typically frown upon Europeans

Despite the evidence that supports assimilation, there is other evidence that does not support this theory. First, Native American tribes generally did not take kindly to the settlement of their land by Europeans, so it is also likely that they would not want to help the settlers.
